# two-qubit-driven-duffing-relaxation# Multiqubit Duffing Dynamics

## Overview

This project investigates relaxation and excitation processes in a system of two interacting superconducting qubits coupled dispersively to a driven nonlinear Duffing oscillator.

The work extends the theory of oscillator-induced relaxation developed for a single qubit to a collective two-qubit system with Ising interaction. Transition rates are computed using Fermi's Golden Rule together with the fluctuation spectrum of a driven Duffing oscillator.

This work was carried out as part of the National Initiative on Undergraduate Science (NIUS) programme under project code T024.

---

## Main Results

- Derived the collective eigenstates and eigenenergies of an interacting two-qubit system.
- Computed oscillator-induced transition rates using Fermi's Golden Rule.
- Identified symmetry-induced selection rules that forbid several collective-state transitions.
- Demonstrated attractor-dependent resonances associated with the quasienergy frequency of the driven Duffing oscillator.
- Investigated the dependence of transition spectra on driving strength, damping, and detuning.
- Observed nonequilibrium population inversion and effective negative temperatures in the driven regime.

---

## Physical Motivation

Driven nonlinear oscillators play a central role in superconducting quantum circuits, particularly in dispersive readout architectures.

When strongly driven, a Duffing oscillator develops bistability and nonequilibrium fluctuations. These fluctuations can induce relaxation and excitation processes in coupled qubits.

For a two-qubit system, collective symmetry introduces additional selection rules absent in the single-qubit case, leading to qualitatively different transition dynamics.
